Conference Format and Schedule

The main international conference in Kathmandu will be preceded by preparatory regional phases to ensure a fully grounded and collaborative programme.

Jump to: Pre-Conference Event Structure Day 1 Day 2 Day 3

Pre-Conference Activities

CESIF will engage regional partners ahead of the main gathering to build a shared foundation of insight and consensus.

Phase I — Regional Strategy

CESIF will convene a regional working group of think tanks from Nepal, India, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, and Myanmar to develop consensus on priority areas regarding China’s engagements.

Phase II — Virtual Dialogues

In collaboration with working group partners, CESIF will organize virtual dialogues among regional stakeholders—including academia, media, civil society, and governments—to hold extensive discussions on country-specific engagements and offer space for comparative analysis. These presentations will form the basis for the main conference design.

Phase III — Main International Conference

A three-day convening in Kathmandu, Nepal with a global delegate mix and an emphasis on regional learning and collective strategy.

  • Proposed dates: 21–23 May 2026 (TBC)
  • Delegates: Experts from South Asia plus China studies specialists from Southeast Asia, Europe, Australia, the US, the UK, Japan, Singapore, and more.

Proposed 3‑Day Event Structure

The conference will feature country-specific presentations and at least 10 panel discussions on identified themes. The first two days will heavily focus on the "China in South Asia" theme.

Key Activities & Formats

  • Panel Discussions: At least 10 dedicated panels exploring multifaceted engagements.
  • Country-Specific Presentations: Highlighting local realities and cross-country learnings.
  • Closed-Door Roundtables: Focused discussions on critical, sensitive issues.
  • High-Level Meetings: Scheduled on-call meetings with Nepali government officials for delegates.

Thematic Focus Areas

  • The Great Power Rivalry in South Asia: Strategic dilemma for small states.
  • China’s peripheral diplomacy: Shifting patterns of cross-border ties.
  • BRI in South Asia: Enthusiasm to skepticism.
  • China’s influence on governance, politics, and institutions.
  • Economic engagements: Debt, transparency, and sovereignty.
  • Information warfare and disinformation campaigns: Raising threat for South Asia.
  • Soft power, cultural influence, and civilizational narratives: A new dependency?
  • Shifting security landscape in South Asia: Navigating China’s security and defense engagements.
